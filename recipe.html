Pumpkin Pie

History of Pumpkin Pie

Pumpkin pie has been enjoyed in North America for hundreds of years. Early American settlers learned from Native Americans how to cook pumpkin and began using it in dishes. By the 1600s, spiced pumpkin fillings became common in pies. Today, pumpkin pie is a traditional dessert served during and other fall celebrations.

About This Recipe

This is a recipe that my brother, my grandma, and me make every time its is someones birthday. My grandma made it withh her grandma too.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin purée
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 unbaked pie crust

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Combine the pumpkin purée, sweetened condensed milk, and eggs in a mixing bowl.
  3. Add cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cloves, and salt. Mix until smooth.
  4. Pour the filling into the unbaked pie crust.
  5. Bake for 15 minutes.
  6. Reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for another 35–40 minutes.
  7. Allow the pie to cool before serving.
